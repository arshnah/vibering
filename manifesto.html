manifesto

you are not a user. you are not a metric. you are a person — and you deserve a place on the internet that is actually yours.

picture it: a page with your name on it, that looks exactly the way you want it to look. a place other people can wander into and get to know you. a place no algorithm can throttle, no feed can bury, and nobody can switch off because “engagement” dipped this quarter.

somewhere to post your projects, rant about your day, keep a guestbook, dump the songs and films and half-finished ideas you love. a room you built by hand, with the door left open.

a personal website is how you get there.

it's the first small act of owning your own patch of the internet. if that idea does anything for you at all — keep reading.

perfect is not the point

the point is ownership, not polish.

make it ugly. make it weird. make it slow. hand-code it in a text editor at 3am and never touch a framework. as long as it's yours and you're a little proud of it, it's right.

“clean” and “professional” are for people getting paid. this is for fun. free yourself of what you think a website is supposed to be, and build one that just sounds like you.

so: stop optimising. make a website you think is cool. then link it into the ring and go wander through everyone else's.
